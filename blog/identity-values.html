Identity and Values

A little exploration and reflection of values and identity

About an ethical decision

I feel very fortunate to have to have a group of good friends who are kind, compassionate, and non judgmental. A few years ago, I kept another, but less close, group of friends who were less open minded and would casually make some pretty toxic remarks. One time at a social gathering, an individual from that group came, and they said some pretty gross things around some friends and my flatmate. After I heard what they said, I was pretty upset and called out the individual. The end result, was I cut contact with the friend group. Reflecting on it now, I'm glad I made the decision to cut ties. However I'm disappointed it took my friends pointing it out, to properly set boundaries and stand up for my values.

Culture shaping identity

Growing up in New Zealand, I've always sensed there's an unwritten rule of being polite above all else. In contrast, the American side of my family would be considered “direct” or even “brash” in NZ. It might almost be considered rude here, and yet, they are such people. It seems both cultures have rubbed off on me, both kindness and honestly are values that are core to me. The tricky bit is trying to balance those two values.

Strengths and weaknesses around learning

My biggest strengths are social intelligence, fairness, and curiosity. Social intelligence and fairness will support me best in group settings, whether it's networking, effective collaboration, or even conflict resolution. Curiosity will help me engage with topics, and can support problem solving and critical thinking, from the desire to explore topics deeply.

Some limitations I really feel are in how well I can maintain focus, and how I find motivation to get tasks completed. But really the biggest limitation for me is my self belief, because of those perceived limitations.

An example of conflict

At my previous job, I had a fairly “abrasive” colleague. The job involved sales and this person was very competitive, in what is usually a harmonious teamwork environment. It was at the point where their competitiveness was at the expense of good relationships with colleagues. It's always my instinct to try to keep the peace, but with the headstrong personalities, mediation didn't improve the situation. Eventually it wore on me too, so I just focused on my own work, as best as I could. In reflection, next time I find a similar situation, I'd like to get to work to understand the individuals motivations and values. I may learn a different perspective, or how to work a little better with them.
