Human Skills, and Foundations Reflection

Human skills learning and the ego

In these 5 weeks of learning with Dev Academy foundation, really everything was completely new for me. It was a new style of learning, a new routine, and all the content I was learning was initially completely foreign. There was a lot of learning about myself too. I was out of my comfort zone of being able to engage with people in person, and my ego convinced me I wasn't up to the task of learning self directed. A big thing I learned is that often a little bit goes a long way. Just a few minutes a day of self reflection, mindfulness, or journaling makes a huge difference for building healthy habits. Even if it doesn't feel like a big step, it takes you a long long way.

Values, Empathy, and Self Awareness in programming

Early on we took some time to examine ourselves and our values. I understood that it was important to do, but not so much initially why in particular for programming. In a professional setting, programming successfully will often be a collaborative effort. Simply having the technical capacity to program well, will not be enough to find success as a programmer. If people enjoy, and are enthusiastic to work with you you,they'll also certainly work better. Self awareness helps you identify your strengths and weaknesses, and even if you're coding alone on a personal project, having empathy and understanding your values helps you realise your biases helps you consider how others might interact with what you're working on.

Surprises and challenges in Human Skills

Something challenging for me while learning human skills was the self examination. Although I am pretty self aware, prodding too deep into myself was pretty uncomfortable at times. Trying to clearly articulate and blog my thoughts was tough for me at times too. I was surprised at some of the problem solving tools. During my research, it seemed that pretty much everyone agreed on some very specific tools and methods to problem solve. I'm still working on using them consistently, but so far they were very helpful.

The role of Human Skills at Dev Academy

I understood coming into Dev Academy that not all the learning material would be technical, but no specifically what the other material would cover. I was pleased to realise that the human skills we've covered are all specific, relevant to programmers, and backed by research.p It makes intuitive sense that employers will be looking for well rounded candidates that will fit in well with their workplace culture and team. I'm not yet so familiar with the industry, but the impression I get is that there are a disproportionate number of developers who work well with code, but just not as well with people. I'm honestly grateful to know that we'll be given the opportunity to become well rounded developers.
