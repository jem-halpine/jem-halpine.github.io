Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

A discussion on Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset, and how it relates to my learning journey

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain and nervous system to change in response to experience. Neuroplasticity takes several different forms, and can happen in response to many things. It can happen from stress or trauma, from learning, or even from injury. May times, neuroplasticity happens without your concious intention. But amazingly, we can make it happen through concious action.


The incredable, also possibly surprising thing, is that we can purposly self-induce neuroplasticity. There is research to incicate that we can support neuroplasticity from:

Learning (eg. a new skill or language)

Getting enough sleep

Mindfulness

Creative pursuits


Understanding that we can have direct input into how our brain functions is incredibly powerful. For me, knowing that I'm not completely at the mercy of how my brain is hardwired, gives me more agency and peace with myself. It also helps me to be conscious what activities will help me and why, and provides more motivation to do those things that will benefit my neuroplasticity. Growth Mindset

Growth Mindset is a concept which was first introduced by psychologist Dr. Carol Dweck, in which the mindset of a person play a huge role in their success. Someone with growth sees attributes such as intelligence or talent, as something that can be improved through effort, rather than simply innate. Developing a growth mindset helps you to appreciate challenges as an opportunity to learn and grow. It is linked to neuroplasticity, as the framework of Growth Mindset is exactly the behavior that leads to neuroplasticity.


Working to develop growth mindset will be a huge boon to my learning strategy. It will help me appreciate any difficult challenges ahead of me. It's very comforting to know that I can improve on aspects of my learning that I see as weaknesses. I'm looking forward to seeing improvement, especially with the things I'm pessimistic about!
