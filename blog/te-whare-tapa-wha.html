Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of wellbeing, based on 4 main pillars of wellbeing. The model is symbolised by the 4 walls of the wharenui, and the connection to the whenua (the land). Using these, I will form a wellbeing plan.

Taha Tinana

Taha Tinana is phyiscal wellbeing. I always make an effort to make decisions that are good for my body, particularly being physically active. Where I most need to improve is ensuring my sleep is good and consistent. I will practice better sleep practices via moderating late caffeine, and early sunlight viewing. I will also commit to cooking my meals rather than buying food, at least 3 days of the week.

Taha Hinengaro

Taha Hinegaro is emotional and mental wellbeing. I believe the best practices for me, will be reflection, mindfulness, and sleep. Reflection and mindfulness are important to help process thoughts and emotions. I will journal at least every two days, and meditate every day. Sleep is something I have struggled with for years, and life is much smoother with decent sleep. I have started tracking my sleep, and when it's poor, looking at what factors might have influenced it.

Taha Wairua

Taha Wairua is spiritual wellbeing. My personal experience of spirituality is connection with the world, to other people, and with myself. Getting caught up in a busy life, I sometimes forget to have appreciation for all those things. I will work on my spiritual wellbeing through mindfulness practice, and expressing my gratitude every day.

Tana Whānau

Tana Whānau is family wellbeing. This encompasses not just related family, but your community at large. I am blessed to have both loving friends and family in my life. My commitment is to spend at least an afternoon with my immediate family per week, and to catch up with a friend, even if just for lunch, at least once per week.

Whenua

The other 4 pillars rest upon the Whenua, the land, as their foundation. I will strengthen my connection with the natural world by getting out for a walk with nature at least twice a week. Mindfulness and meditation also brings me an appreciation and connection to the natural world.
